# The Sundering of Aethermoor — World Background

## The Age Before the Sundering

In the time before memory, the world of **Aethermoor** was whole.

A single continent stretched from the volcanic southern peaks to the frozen northern wastes, bound together by an invisible weave of magic that suffused the land, the sea, and the sky. At the center of this weave existed the **Worldstone** — a crystalline spire the height of a mountain, neither carved nor crafted, simply *there*, as if the world itself had exhaled it into being. The Worldstone was pure creation magic made solid: reality's anchor, the source from which all life flowed and to which all death returned.

For ten thousand years, the people of Aethermoor lived in a golden age. The **Five Peoples** — the dwarves of the deep forges, the elves of the frost-rivers, the forest-kin of the ancient groves, the Aetherwings of the high winds, and the humans of the middle-lands — knew war, knew grief, knew joy, but always the Worldstone's light touched the horizon at dawn, reminding them that the world endured.

---

## The Fall of Malachar

Not all who lived in Aethermoor's golden age were content.

**Malachar** was once the greatest of the god-touched Riftwalkers — a mortal mage elevated to near-divine power through decades of study at the Worldstone's base. He called himself a philosopher, but he was something stranger: a man who believed that existence itself was a prison.

*"The Worldstone does not anchor us,"* he wrote, in his infamous treatise, *The Book of Unraveling*. *"It chains us. Every life is born into the same world, dies in the same world, returns to the same world. There is no escape. There is no elsewhere. Only the Void beyond reality's edge offers true freedom — the freedom of ending, of unbeing."*

His colleagues dismissed him. Then feared him. Then it was too late.

In the year now called **the Shattering**, Malachar ascended to the Worldstone's spire alone. He carried a weapon he had spent thirty years forging: the **Void Spike**, a shard of pure anti-creation, harvested from the place beyond the edges of reality. He drove it into the Worldstone's heart.

The explosion was not of fire or sound. It was of *meaning*. The Worldstone shattered into five pieces — each a Shard glowing with the pale light of unmade potential. The pieces flew to the five corners of the world, and as they separated, Aethermoor *tore*.

The single continent ripped along five fault lines of magic, each fragment drifting into its own bubble of fragmented reality: the **Five Realms**, each slowly losing coherence, slowly fraying at the edges where the Void crept in.

Malachar himself was consumed by the explosion — or so everyone believed. His body was never found.

## The Five Realms

Each Realm is a fragment of what Aethermoor once was. They are connected only by **Rift Gates** — tears in reality that a trained Riftwalker can open and step through. Without the Worldstone, these gates are unstable and cost immense magical energy to use.

Every Realm is dying. The Void — the cold nothingness beyond all existence — presses in at every edge. In the sky, in the deep earth, in the spaces between heartbeats, you can feel it: the slow erasure. Most people have simply stopped looking at the horizon.

### Emberveil

*"The fire does not warm here. It remembers warmth."*

A realm of perpetual volcanic activity. The sun is barely visible through the ash-haze. Mountains of black glass and rivers of cooling lava define a landscape that is brutal, beautiful, and unforgiving. The great **Forge-Cities** of the dwarves cling to the caldera rims — massive industrial fortresses whose hammering never stops, day or night, as if the dwarves believe that if they stop working, the world will remember to end.

The Emberveil Shard fell into the deepest magma chamber of the **Ironmaw Volcano**, fusing with the rock and heat. It blazes like a second sun from the depths. The dwarves know it is there. They will not let outsiders near it.

**Void Manifestation here:** Emberveil's Void corruption appears as sudden silence — fires going out, the hammering stopping, the volcanic heat turning cold. When the silence spreads, people disappear.

### Thornwood

*"The forest remembers everything. That is why it weeps."*

An ancient forest so vast and dense that sunlight reaches the floor only in isolated shafts, like gold wire threading through a dark tapestry. The trees here are primordial — some as wide as villages, their root systems forming tunnels and caverns. The forest is alive in ways that defy simple magic: it *thinks*, it *feels*, and it does not trust strangers.

The nature spirits of Thornwood — the **Greensoul** — are neither friendly nor hostile. They are *territorial*. They are also frightened, though they would never admit it. The Shard fell into the **Heartroot** — the oldest living tree at the center of the forest, a god-tree wider than a city and older than memory.

**Void Manifestation here:** Trees rot from within — not the slow decay of death, but instant blackening, as if the life is simply *removed*. The rotted trees then move. They do not like living things.

### Crystalmere

*"We measure time in the thickness of ice. We have been measuring a long time."*

A realm of perpetual winter, where the surface is a blasted tundra of white stone and howling wind. But beneath — beneath is a wonder. An entire underground sea, frozen solid over millennia, but so crystalline and clear that you can see leagues in every direction through the ice. The **Frostborn elves** built their civilization *inside* this sea, carving cities into the crystal, their buildings lit by bioluminescent algae trapped in ice pockets for ten thousand years.

The Crystalmere Shard fell into the deepest trench of the crystal sea and flash-froze a cathedral of ice around itself. The Frostborn consider it a holy relic and will not touch it — they believe disturbing it will end their world. They may be right.

**Void Manifestation here:** The crystal sea cracks. The cracks show not more ice underneath, but absolute darkness — the Void itself pressing up from below. When a crack reaches a Frostborn city, the city blinks out of existence. Gone, as if it was never there.

### The Ashen Wastes

*"There was a city here once. I remember it every time I close my eyes."*

Of all the Realms, the Ashen Wastes is the most damaged. It was the first to feel Malachar's influence after the Shattering — some say he retreated here to lick his wounds, and his presence corrupted the land permanently.

Nothing living grows in the Ashen Wastes. The sky is a permanent twilight of grey and purple. The ruins of a great human civilization stretch in every direction — empty towers, collapsed cathedrals, roads that go nowhere. And walking among the ruins: the dead. Not the simple undead of necromancer magic, but something worse — **echoes**, people still going through the motions of their final moments, forever, unaware they are dead.

The Shard here is held by **Malachar's Shadow** — whether a remnant of Malachar himself, a summoned proxy, or something new that has taken root in his name, none can say for certain. The Ashen Wastes Shard must be *taken*, not found.

**Void Manifestation here:** This Realm is already 60% consumed by the Void. The edges of the map simply end in absolute darkness. It grows larger every day.

### Skyreach

*"We fly because the ground betrays you. The sky does not lie."*

Floating islands, each a mile or more across, drifting in a stratospheric band above the clouds. No one below can see them — they exist above the cloud layer, in permanent golden sunlight while the world below is cold and grey. The **Aetherwings** are the sky people: light-framed, keen-eyed, with vestigial feathered wings on their arms that let them glide between islands on the thermal currents.

Skyreach was least damaged by the Sundering and the Aetherwings know it. They have chosen isolation — they destroyed their Rift Gates and let the lower Realms fend for themselves. Some of them feel guilt about this. Many do not.

The Shard is in the possession of **Sky-Admiral Zephyra**, though she doesn't fully understand what she has. She found it tumbling from the sky like a falling star and keeps it as a trophy.

**Void Manifestation here:** Islands simply fall. Without warning, without reason — a mile-wide chunk of rock and civilization drops below the clouds into oblivion. The Aetherwings have lost three islands in the past year.

## The Riftwalkers

The Riftwalkers were once an order — a hundred strong, trained at the Worldstone's base, keepers of the passages between the Realm-fragments after the Sundering. They believed they could hold the world together by keeping communication and trade open between the Realms.

Malachar, or his agents, hunted them systematically. The order is down to a handful now, scattered and in hiding. The player is one of the last.

**Riftwalking mechanics:** Opening a Rift Gate requires **Rift Points** — the internal reserve of creation magic that every Riftwalker carries. It is spent on opening gates and partially restored by resting near Shard energy. Without Rift Points, a Riftwalker is stranded.

**Rift Sense:** Riftwalkers can feel the Void — its creeping advancement, the locations of Shard energy, and the distortions in reality that mark the locations of lost Rift Gates. This sense grows stronger as more Shards are collected.

---

## The Void

The Void is not evil in any meaningful sense. It has no agenda, no hatred, no desire. It simply *is* — the cold absence that exists outside reality's edges, pressing in wherever reality weakens.

The Sundering cracked reality. Every day, every hour, the cracks grow. The Void presses through. When the Void touches a living thing, it does not kill it in the normal sense — it *removes* it, as if it was never there. No body, no ghost, no memory in anyone who loved the person. Just absence.

The **Void Corruption counter** in gameplay represents how far the Void has advanced. At 100%, Aethermoor ceases to exist.

---

## The Worldstone Shards

Each Shard retains a fragment of the Worldstone's creative power. They pulse with light that cannot be extinguished — even in the heart of the Ashen Wastes, they glow. A trained Riftwalker can attune to a Shard and absorb its power.

When all five Shards are brought together, they can be reforged into the Worldstone — but only in the right place, at the right time, with the right knowledge. The last sage who knows the reforging ritual is **Seraphel** of Crystalmere.

Malachar knows this. He will do everything in his power to prevent the reforging.

---

## Malachar the Unbound

Whether Malachar survived the Shattering as a physical being, a spirit, or something in between is unclear. What is clear: he is not done.

He appears as visions — tall, thin, robed in black so dark it seems to absorb light, his face a mask of absolute serenity. He speaks in a calm, almost gentle voice. He does not consider himself a villain. He considers himself the only person willing to end suffering by ending existence itself.

He has agents in every Realm. He has had centuries to prepare. And he holds one Shard himself.

His goal is not to stop you specifically — it is to ensure the Worldstone is never reforged. If the Void consumes Aethermoor before you succeed, he considers that a victory for freedom.

*"I don't want you to die,"* he told a Riftwalker once, before unmade her. *"I want you to stop existing. It's gentler. You'll understand, at the end."*

| Year 412 — The Silence | The Riftwalker Order goes dark; last coordinated communication is a warning signal |
