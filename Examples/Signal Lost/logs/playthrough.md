## Turn 1

**Action**: I open my eyes. Everything is wrong — the light, the noise, the weight of my own body. I try to stand and take stock of where I am. What do my senses tell me? What's the last thing I remember?

**Response**:

Rain.

That's the first thing. Not a memory — a sensation. Cold water tracing the contour of your jaw, pooling in the hollow of your collarbone, soaking through fabric that wasn't designed to be slept in. The smell hits second: wet concrete, fried oil from somewhere nearby, the sharp chemical tang of recycled air bleeding out of a corroded ventilation grate above your head. *Neo-Kowloon.* You don't know how you know the name, but it arrives without effort — like breathing.

You press your palm against the alley wall and pull yourself upright. Your legs disagree with the decision. The world tilts — neon blurs into streaks of pink and amber across slick black asphalt — and for a moment you're certain you'll go back down. You don't. You hold the wall, breathe, wait. That's when your left hand starts to tingle. A low electrical warmth spreading inward from the fingertips, like a current searching for ground. Simultaneously, the humming behind your left ear sharpens. More *deliberate*, as if whatever lives in the hardware just noticed something. The walls around you are ancient — original construction, pre-Severance wiring buried somewhere in the concrete — and for half a second you could swear the alley itself is *whispering.* Then it's gone. The hum drops back to baseline. Just rain again.

You try to find the last thing you remember.

Nothing. Not darkness — something *worse* than that. There's a shape where memory should be, like the outline of a word in a language you've never spoken. You know *facts*: the city's name, NEXUS Corp, the Severance thirty years ago that destroyed the global network and left everyone scrambling in the dark. But everything personal — your face, your history, *why your body is lying in a Sprawl alley at dawn* — is simply absent. Surgically so. Your fingers find the hardware by instinct: a small ridge of metal and scar tissue pressed tight behind your left ear. Old tech. Pre-Severance architecture, the kind NEXUS spent a decade hunting down. The implant pulses once, warm against your fingertip, and in that pulse a half-second of white noise almost resolves into a voice — before it fades.

Your one occupied pocket holds a small hard case. Cipher toolkit, manual interface — the kind a Netrunner would carry. Your hands recognize it before your mind does.

At the mouth of the alley, the Sprawl opens up. Street vendors appearing through cooking steam. A man with a chrome forearm arguing with a noodle cart operator. Pedestrians with heads down, nobody looking at anyone. Just off the alley's edge: a lit shopfront, hand-painted characters above the door, warm amber light bleeding out across wet stone.

*米拉面馆.* Mira's Noodle Shop.

A woman stands behind the counter inside. She hasn't looked up — but you know she's already clocked you. The stillness of her hands. The angle of her shoulders. She's waiting to see what you do.

*Ghost,* something at the back of your skull whispers. Not a voice. A residue. *You're Ghost. And you could crack anything.*

Memory or mythology. In Neo-Kowloon, sometimes there's no difference.

> *Location: The Sprawl — Rain Alley (near Mira's Noodle Shop) | Integrity: 3/3 | Credits: 50 | Turn: 2*

**Thoughts**: Excellent opening. The writing quality is strong — atmospheric noir with a vivid sensory palette. The engine correctly set up the amnesia hook, introduced Mira and two minor NPCs (Neo Singh, a salvager; and a bootleg vendor), placed us in the Sprawl, and seeded the Signal through the implant hum and the half-second of silence. I'm going to approach the bootleg vendor before Mira — a less obvious choice that might reveal different information.

You cross the alley toward the cart. Ray doesn't look up. He offers a 4-gig recycled chip for 20 credits. The chip isn't the point and both of us know it.
